Overview of the MPT-SST Framework

The Metaphysical Peace Theory and Spiritual-Structural Transformation (MPT-SST) is a universal peace framework developed by Aliyu Sani Kafinga between 2024 and 2025. It advances peacebuilding beyond conventional diplomatic and structural models by placing consciousness at the center of conflict transformation.

MPT-SST recognizes that sustainable peace cannot be achieved solely through institutions, negotiations or policies. Peace emerges from coherence between inner consciousness, cultural meaning systems and external social structures.

Core Principles of MPT-SST

Consciousness as the Primary Arena

Conflict originates within consciousness and peace must first be cultivated internally before structural resolution can occur.

Spiritual-Structural Coherence

Lasting peace arises when spiritual values and social systems are aligned rather than separated.

Multi-Peace Track Synergy

Peace is achieved through the integration of diplomacy, community engagement, education, culture, spirituality and governance.

Linguistic and Cultural Integrity

Peace concepts must retain their metaphysical depth across languages and cultures.

Metaphysical Peace Vocabulary and Consciousness Translation

The Metaphysical Peace Vocabulary of Consciousness (MPVOC) preserves peace concepts from diverse linguistic and cultural traditions while maintaining their spiritual and ethical depth.

Examples include Arabic Sulha, Hausa Lumana and other indigenous peace expressions whose transformative meaning is often lost in literal translation.

Where MPT-SST Applies

  • Political and governance conflicts
  • Geopolitical and international tensions
  • Economic injustice and structural violence
  • Ethno-religious and cultural conflicts
  • Community and societal fragmentation
  • Diplomatic mediation and negotiations
  • Education and consciousness transformation
  • Environmental and planetary peace challenges

Framework Status

MPT-SST is a living and evolving peace framework shaped by ongoing scholarly research, field engagement and consciousness-based inquiry.
